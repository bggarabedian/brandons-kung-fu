# Code of Conduct

This is a public, beginner-friendly project. Keep it a place people are glad to
contribute to.

## Expected behavior

- Be **respectful** — assume good faith; disagree on ideas, not people.
- Be **constructive** — when you flag a problem, point at a fix or a next step.
- Be **safety-minded** — this kit is about not letting agents make silent
  repo/security/public-posture mistakes; carry that care into the community too.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks.
- Doxxing or publishing anyone's private information.
- Abuse, threats, or sustained disruption.
- Spam or off-topic promotion.
- Posting other people's secrets, credentials, or private data.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may **edit, hide, close, lock, or remove** any contribution, and may
**block** accounts, to keep the project healthy. Decisions are at the maintainers'
discretion.

## Reporting

Report a problem through **GitHub's own reporting/moderation tools**, or by opening
or commenting through the project's **public GitHub channels** (an issue or a PR
comment) — without exposing anyone's private details. If a report itself would
require sharing private information, say that you have something to report and ask
a maintainer for a private channel rather than posting the details publicly.

This document is authored fresh for this project; it does not adopt or reproduce
any third-party code-of-conduct text.
